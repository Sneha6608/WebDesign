From Looms to Legends :

Discover Dhalapathar's Craft

Dhalapathar is known for its GI-tagged Parda Art and traditional weaving, started in 1936. Using mercerized yarn and chemical dyes, weavers create coarse-textured fabrics with large motifs of flowers, figures, and myths through a unique double weft technique.

Creative & Cultural

Step Into the Fabric of Folklore — Explore the Motifs of Dhalapathar

Pakshi

"Chada Pakhi" often refers to stylized flying birds depicted in symmetrical or geometric forms.

Manaba(human)

These motifs often depict folk life, tribal dances, farming scenes, or ritualistic postures. They are usually stylized and geometric, resembling simplified stick figures or symmetrical forms.

Phula

Floral motifs in stylized or geometric form.Represents beauty, nature, and prosperity

Kumbha (Temple Motif)

A triangular or stepped pattern representing the spire of a temple. Symbolizes spirituality and Odisha’s temple architecture.
