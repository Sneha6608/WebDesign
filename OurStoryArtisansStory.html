The Hands That

Weave History

"The soul of Dhalapathar Ikat lies in the hands of its weavers. Passed down through generations, these artisans carry stories stitched in time — of resilience, tradition, and artistry. This section celebrates their voices and lives."

Praful Ku. Singh

Age: 60 Years
Experience: 50 Years
Main Products: Sarees, Gamjha, Duaptta & dress Material

“Weaving is in our blood,” he says, recalling how the craft has been passed down since his grandfather’s time. Today, his entire family is involved in weaving and relies on handloom saree sales for their livelihood. To support the family during slow seasons, he also runs a small water-supply tank business. Earlier, a middleman handled their sales, but during the COVID lockdowns, supply chains broke and raw materials ran out. With time on his hands and determination in his heart, he launched “Draupadi Handlooms,” now supplying sarees to shops across Odisha. They once wove kusumi sarees, known for their beauty and high value, but had to stop due to the unavailability of kusumi threads. Still, the loom keeps spinning with hope and heritage.

Nirmala Devi

Age: 60 Years
Experience: 60 Years
Main Products: Sarees and Gamjha

She joined the village weaving center with the hope of making new friends and building a sense of belonging. Her inspiration sparked when she saw her neighbor skillfully working at the loom, and her curiosity to understand the intricate beauty of the craft began to grow. Every morning, she rises early, tidies her home with care, and heads off to the weaving unit with quiet determination. There, she spends her day learning to weave sarees, towels, and gamchas, each thread bringing her closer to the heritage she once only observed. In the evenings, she returns home with tired hands but a joyful heart, resting with a sense of fulfillment. Now, as she continues to learn and grow in this art form, she feels not only skilled but also deeply connected to her culture, her community, and her newfound friends.

Mayadhar Sahoo

Age: 60 Years
Experience: 50 Years
Main Products :Sarees, Gamjha, Duaptta & dress Material

All family members are involved in the handloom process and business that his grandfather started. He learned the craft during his leisure time from his father. In past years a marketing middleman was supplying Kusumi sarees and Bounki pata nationally and internationally. He almost makes one saree per day with 2/3 days of preparing time of threads and sends 20 sarees to a middleman called Mahajan for which he is paid a daily wage of Rs 300/400. Production is affected or slowed during monsoon season due to issues with the drying of threads that are soaked in dye.

Gadadhar Sahoo

Age: 60 Years
Experience: 50 Years
Main Products: Sarees, Gamjha, Duaptta & dress Material

For over two centuries, his family has been deeply rooted in the art of Dhalapathar handloom weaving — a legacy that began long before his grandparents. Today, the tradition proudly continues, with his wife, son, and daughter-in-law all actively involved in the craft. The women of the household meticulously handle the thread preparation, while the men dedicate themselves to the rhythmic beauty of weaving. His hands move with ease and familiarity, and remarkably, he experiences no physical discomfort a testament to years of devotion and muscle memory. For him, weaving is more than a livelihood; it is an inseparable part of his identity and a thread that connects generations of stories, resilience, and pride.

Sarojini Rana

Age: 60 Years
Experience: 50 Years
Main Products: Sarees, Gamjha, Duaptta & dress Material

She is continuing the tradition of handloom work passed in her family by joining the Ngo company. All her family members are involved in the handloom craft. In the beginning, she faced issues with back and neck aches but now has gotten comfortable working with handloom. She learns to produce saree, towel and gamcha
